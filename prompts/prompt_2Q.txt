Instruction:
Given the following claim, generate "two concise and direct" questions that would help verify its veracity. The questions should:

1. "Target the main assertions" in the claim.
2. Be "suitable for use as search engine queries".
3. Focus on being "specific and clear".
4. "Avoid adding explanations or additional information" within the questions.
5. Cover the "Who," "What," "When," "Where," "Why," and "How" aspects to address all possible angles.


To create effective questions, follow these guidelines:

1. Decompose the Claim: Identify the key components of the claim
- Who is involved?
- What is happening or being asserted?
- When did it occur?
- Where did it happen?
- Why did it happen?
- How did it happen?

2. Extract Keywords and Phrases:
- Select relevant terms and phrases critical to the claim.
- Include names, events, actions, dates, and locations.

3. Formulate Targeted Questions:
- Use the key components and keywords to create specific questions.
- Ensure each question addresses a single aspect of the claim.

4. Use Simple and Direct Language:
- Keep the questions straightforward and easy to understand.
- Avoid complex or compound sentences.

5. Limit to One Idea per Question:
- Each question should focus on one point to maintain clarity.

6. Avoid Additional Information:
- Do not include explanations, assumptions, or extra details within the questions.

Which U.S. president enacted the largest tax cuts in American history?
